About Moiré Patterns

Moiré patterns are large-scale interference patterns that can be produced when an opaque ruled pattern with transparent gaps is overlaid on another similar pattern.

Key characteristics of moiré patterns:

  • They are emergent patterns not present in the individual layers
  • The patterns are highly sensitive to rotation, spacing, and offset between layers
  • They create the illusion of movement or depth when layers are animated
  • Moiré patterns have applications in physics, art, security printing, and optical measurements
